ShowWork

Designing assignments that support responsible AI use

Why this tool exists −

In the past, our assignments were largely about output, and the process a student used to get there was usually evident in that output. A clear, well-reasoned plan implied a clear, well-reasoned process behind it. Now that students have powerful tools to help produce that output, the old assumption breaks down. A polished deliverable no longer tells us, on its own, how the student got there, or whether the competencies we are trying to assess were exercised.

A familiar reframe: Early in my career, I worked with children with special needs, and I kept a reminder on my bulletin board: “Don’t ask if this child is smart, ask how this child is smart.” A similar shift applies here. The question is not whether a student used AI, but how do we help this student demonstrate the specific competencies this assignment is meant to reveal.

This reframe changes the academic integrity conversation too. Reasoning-visible assignments shift the central question from “did this student use AI?” (faculty proving an absence) to “did this student demonstrate the competency?” (faculty assessing visible reasoning).

ShowWork helps you apply that question to a real assignment. Paste in your assignment and rubric, and ShowWork reviews it against nine diagnostic patterns across three domains of responsible AI use — returning what your assignment already does well and the highest-leverage opportunity to strengthen it further.

ShowWork was based on the work by Jean Gordon, Eric Richardson, Dawn Oetjen, Reid Oetjen, and Nikki Strawn, from the AUPHA Art of Teaching Institute course “Future Fluent by Design: Reimagining Assessments for the Age of AI” (Medical University of South Carolina and University of Central Florida).

How to use this tool −
  1. Fill in what you have. The more context you provide, the more specific the review. At minimum, paste your assignment prompt. Adding the rubric, purpose, program context, and competency will sharpen every finding.
  2. Choose a review method. Use your own AI account (Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini) to run the review here, or copy a ready-made prompt to use in any AI tool you already have. Formatted ShowWork cards work best with Claude. If you use ChatGPT, type "Make this output reader friendly" in ChatGPT to get a readable result there.
  3. Read the strengths first. ShowWork will tell you what your assignment is already doing well before identifying anything to improve. Take that seriously — these are things worth preserving.
  4. One opportunity per domain. ShowWork surfaces the highest-leverage opportunity in each of the three domains. This is the change most likely to move the needle. Running your assignment again after revising may surface a different opportunity in that domain — that’s by design.
  5. Understanding your score. ShowWork scores each pattern from 1 to 3. Your total out of 27 reflects how well the assignment currently supports responsible AI use. 22 or above (80%+) means the assignment is already doing most things well — focus on preserving strengths. 14 to 21 (50–79%, Developing) means there are meaningful opportunities worth exploring. Below 14 (under 50%) suggests the assignment may benefit from a more intentional redesign conversation.
  6. Works on drafts too. If you are still designing a course, running an early draft through ShowWork can help shape the assignment from the start.
